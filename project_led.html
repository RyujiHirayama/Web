Development of volumetric display based on multi-bit color LED

Ryuji Hirayama, Hirotaka Nakayama, Atsushi Shiraki, Takashi Kakue, Tomoyoshi Shimobaba, and Tomoyoshi Ito,
APCCAS 2014, Okinawa, Japan (Nov. 17-20, 2014). [link]


Abstract

We made a volumetric display consisting of 8 x 8 x 8 full-color light-emitting diodes (LEDs), and demonstrate that it can exhibit two dynamic color images in different directions. In addition, we designed and developed the new control circuit of the LED-based volumetric display to exhibit full-color dynamic 3D images. The circuit was implemented on a field programmable gate array (FPGA) and achieved pulse width modulation, which requires high-speed processing. We experimentally evaluated the developed system by measuring the luminance of an LED with varying the input and confirmed that the system works appropriately. As a result, we succeeded in demonstrating that the volumetric display exhibits different full-color dynamic 2D images in two orthogonal directions.
