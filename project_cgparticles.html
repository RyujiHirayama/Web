Controllable color particles in a 3D crystal projecting multiple dynamic full-color images

Ryuji Hirayama, Hirotaka Nakayama, Atsushi Shiraki, Takashi Kakue, Tomoyoshi Shimobaba, and Tomoyoshi Ito
ACM SIGGRAPH Posters, Los Angeles, USA (July 30 - Aug. 3, 2017). [link]


Abstract

In this project, we demonstrate a 3D structure projecting multiple dynamic full-color images in different directions. The 3D structure is designed by the algorithm proposed in the previous project, and is represented as a crowd of controllable color particles rendered in a cylindrical 3D crystal with a CG software. We confirmed that five dynamic full-color images could be successfully observed from different viewpoints of the rendered 3D structure. Although the 3D structure is rendered with CG in this project, rendering the designed 3D structure in the real world by using volumetric displays enables our method to be applied to the information service systems including digital signage as well as a steganographic media art, which can provide fun of finding hidden information.
