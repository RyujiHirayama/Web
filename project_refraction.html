Refraction-compensating algorithm for a 3D glass structure exhibiting multiple 2D images

Ryuji Hirayama, Atsushi Shiraki, Hirotaka Nakayama, Takashi Kakue, Tomoyoshi Shimobaba, and Tomoyoshi Ito,
Frontiers in Optics / Laser Science 2016, Rochester, USA (Oct. 17-21, 2016). [link]


Abstract

In this project, we propose a refraction-compensating algorithm for a 3-D crystal with a curved surface (e.g. cylinder and sphere) projecting multiple 2-D images. This algorithm can improve the image quality of the projected images by compensating the image distortion caused by the refraction, and can remove the conventional constraint on the projection directions. Namely, the projection directions from the 3D crystal with a curved surface can be set at 180-degree-opposite to each other. We indeed manufactured a cylindrical 3-D crystal projecting eight images, consequently confirmed that all the images successfully can be observed.
